The Tapestry

Every natural law — and every frontier — from first principles

Every law of nature rests on a simpler one beneath it. Logic lets us think. Numbers let us count. Calculus lets us describe change. Symmetry gives us conservation. Conservation gives us Newton. Newton gives us orbits and Einstein. And on, all the way up to life, minds and galaxies.

This is the map. Foundations at the centre. Each ring outward builds on what came before. At the very top, in dashed circles, sits the Frontier — things humanity doesn't know yet. Because the iceberg has an underside.

The Tapestry shows ~40 well-established natural laws — the "tip" we understand. Above them, dashed into the fog, sits the Frontier: eleven explicitly-named open problems (quantum gravity, dark matter and dark energy nature, the measurement problem, the origin of life, consciousness, fine-tuning, the arrow of time, baryogenesis, P vs NP, and "what we don't know we don't know").

How big is the iceberg really? Nobody knows. Every era of science has been blindsided by discoveries it had no category for — quantum mechanics in 1900, DNA in 1953, dark energy in 1998. Our era is almost certainly no different. Optimistic estimates put our understanding of the fundamental physics at ~90% for accessible energies, and our understanding of complex systems, minds and origins at perhaps 5–15%. The honest answer: we know the tip, and the underside keeps growing as we learn.
